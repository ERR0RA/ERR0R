Solving One‑Step and Two‑Step Equations

An equation is like a balance. Whatever you do to one side, you must also do to the other side.

One‑Step Equations

These only need one operation to solve.

Example: Solve \(x + 7 = 12\)

Subtract 7 from both sides → \(x = 5\)

Two‑Step Equations

These need two operations.

Example: Solve \(3x - 4 = 11\)

Add 4 → \(3x = 15\)

Divide by 3 → \(x = 5\)

Practice Questions (Year 8)

  1. \(x + 9 = 14\)
  2. \(2x - 3 = 11\)
  3. \(5x + 2 = 27\)
  4. \(x - 8 = 3\)
  5. \(4x + 1 = 17\)

Extension Questions (GCSE Grade 4–5)

  1. \(3(x - 2) = 15\)
  2. \(2x + 5 = 3x - 7\)
  3. \(4(x + 1) - 3 = 2x + 9\)

Expanding Brackets

Single Brackets

Multiply everything inside the bracket by the number or letter outside.

Example: \(4(x + 3) = 4x + 12\)

Double Brackets (FOIL)

Multiply:

  • First
  • Outer
  • Inner
  • Last

Example: \((x + 2)(x + 5)\)

→ \(x^2 + 7x + 10\)

Practice Questions (Year 8)

  1. \(3(x + 4)\)
  2. \(5(2x - 1)\)
  3. \(7(x + 3)\)
  4. \((x + 2)(x + 3)\)
  5. \((x + 5)(x - 1)\)

Extension Questions (GCSE Grade 5–6)

  1. \((2x - 3)(x + 4)\)
  2. \((x - 5)(x - 2)\)
  3. \(3(x + 1)(x - 2)\)

Laws of Indices

Indices (powers) follow special rules:

  • \(a^m \times a^n = a^{m+n}\)
  • \(a^m \div a^n = a^{m-n}\)
  • \((a^m)^n = a^{mn}\)
  • \(a^0 = 1\)
  • \(a^{-m} = \frac{1}{a^m}\)
  • \(a^{m/n} = \sqrt[n]{a^m}\)

  2. \(x^7 \div x^4\)
  3. \((y^2)^3\)
  4. \(b^{-2}\)
  5. \(16^{1/2}\)

Experimental Probability

Experimental probability is based on results from an experiment.

Formula:

\(\text{Probability} = \frac{\text{Number of favourable outcomes}}{\text{Total trials}}\)

Example: A dice is rolled 60 times and lands on 6 ten times.

Probability = \(10/60 = 1/6\)

Practice Questions (Year 8)

  1. You flip a coin 20 times and get 8 heads. What is the probability of heads?
  2. A spinner is spun 50 times and lands on red 15 times. Probability of red?
  3. A bag has 3 green, 5 blue, 2 black counters. What is the probability of green or blue?
  4. Out of 200 students, 60 walk to school. What is the probability a student walks?
  5. A number generator picks numbers 1–10. It lands on 7 four times out of 30 tries. Probability?

Extension Questions (GCSE Grade 4–5)

  1. A biased coin lands on heads 70 out of 100 times. What is the probability of tails?
  2. A dice is rolled 120 times. It lands on even numbers 78 times. Probability of odd?
  3. A class of 30 students: 12 play football, 8 play basketball, 5 play both. What is the probability a student plays at least one sport?
